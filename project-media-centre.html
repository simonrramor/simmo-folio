    Why wont people make my stuff
Qubit positions itself as an industry leader in e-commerce personalisation. As a part of this, Qubit produces a content for a variety of industry verticals. This includes videos, webinars, events, ebooks, infographics, blog posts, case studies and more.

We wanted to make the content more accessible by consolidating these resources into a one stop shop. The challenge was to create a vast feed of information while still being usable. Users should be able to find what they are looking for based on topic and verticals as well as stumble upon new and exciting information while browsing. The goal was to create an information hub that users would return to for the latest insights into the personalisation and marketing industry.
Due to the large amount of content, the search feature was a priority. We implemented a search and filtering system front and center of the page.
Valuable content is categorised by industry vertical and media type in a feed. To make sure popular and important articles can be found easily, they can be highlighted for priority viewing. This also allows Qubit to push the latest and more relevant content to its users.
Qubit produces videos on special topics or for events. Knowing that video content engages users more than articles, we created feature blocks to allow users to easily watch through a whole set of videos one after another without leaving the page.s
To help users find the most relevant content, we include mouse overlays summarising the content they are about to click through which helps manage expectations. Highlighting the amount of shares adds an element of social proof, showing the user the popularity of the content before they decide to consuming it.
Prime time for reading content is during the morning and evening commute. We created a mobile version of the webpage allowing users to easily consume content on the go.
