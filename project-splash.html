    The Problem with Internal Communication
Qubit has offices around the world which makes spreading news a challenge. Remote workers and distant offices can often feel left out of company culture and news. We were tasked with finding a solution to that problem. Get a deep dive on my thinking on this problem here on my blog.
The solution was an Internal communications portal, to be installed as a start-up page as well as homepage. This allows the news to be viewed easily and often, in a location that people were already visit. Priority news is front and center of the design, to ensure no important messages are missed. Users receive a personalized welcome message and beautiful background imagery pulled from Google chromecast. To help with adoption, we added time zones of all Qubit offices across the bottom of the screen. This also helps people when booking in meetings across the globe.
For Qubit employees to gain a clear view of all company activities, the newsfeed is designed to be updated by admins from every department all around the world. To help with adoption, commonly used links were added at the top of the page.
As the portal holds sensitive information one of the requirements was limit access to Qubit employees only. We met that requirement using Google Single Sign On, so only people with a Qubit email address can log on.
As the portal is the Chrome Browser Homepage, employees could accidentally reveal confidential information when presenting to clients. We added a privacy switch that removes all potentially sensitive material from the screen so employees can use their laptops in public and with clients, without worrying about showing confidential information.
To facilitate remote working, we also created a mobile version of the portal via a URL.

To find out more about my process and the highs and lows of this project please read my blog post 'The problem with internal communication'
