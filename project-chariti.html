    Chariti App
  • Platform
    App concept & prototype
  • Team
    Simon Amor (Designer)
After moving to London, I decided I wanted to do something good in my free time by participating in charity work and volunteering in community projects. It didn't take long for me to realise just how hard it was finding local charity events, let alone sign up for them. I decided to design and prototype a concept event based app to solve the problem of finding charity and volunteer work in your area.
Sign up process is quick and simple, allowing people to start looking for events as soon as possible. Logging in via facebook allows users to instantly access the app and use their facebook information to fill out their profile.
With permission, the app will locate the user's current position. From this the users can then select a search radius in wich they would like to attend events.
An events screen shows all the upcoming events within the search radius. Users can filter by popular events, dates the events are on, the location of events or even events friends are going to. Users can also seen events coming up that they have registerd for, If they are yet to register for an event they will be prompted to find one on the search screen.
Free time is rare, and people like to plan ahead. Users can select dates they wish to attend events in advance for a more targeted search.
A comment section allows for people to ask questions to hosts or other users. A rating system allows the most usefull questions to raise to the top, surfacing them for other users.
To gameafy the processe and to help with participant selection added a rating system. This allows for event organisers to give Kudos to helpful attendees. As events are often overbooked it lets hosts select the best people to help out and encourages positive behaviour.
Awards can be given by event hosts for exceptional helpfulness. Awards are also automatically received for compleating various milestones in-app. This continues the gamification of the app and helps increase retention.
